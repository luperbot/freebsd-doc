FreeBSD Internal Pages
======================

This page contains pointers to pages documenting aspects of the FreeBSD
project itself, as viewed separately from the codebase.

Projects
--------

There are various `projects which lie outside the mainstream development
path <../projects/projects.html>`__, in which a number of groups are
working on the 'cutting edge' to expand FreeBSD's range of capabilities
in new directions.

Here is an overview of the `FreeBSD Release Engineering
Process <../doc/en_US.ISO8859-1/articles/releng/article.html>`__.

And here you will find the `schedules for upcoming official releases of
FreeBSD. <../releng/index.html>`__

Policies
--------

To avoid chaos with a project this large spread out all over the globe,
there have to be some `Policies for FreeBSD
Committers <policies.html>`__.

People
------

The FreeBSD?Project Staff consists of `teams, groups and
individuals <../administration.html>`__ with designated project roles
and areas of responsibility and the
`developers <../doc/en/articles/contributors/staff-committers.html>`__.
By following the links, one will find a list of them, as well as
explanations of who is responsible for what.

You can read here core's `Hat Term Limits Policy <hats.html>`__ and some
guidelines from Warner Losh on `how to work with
hats <working-with-hats.html>`__.

Resources
---------

Here is a list of some `technical resources for FreeBSD
committers <developer.html>`__.

For those with accounts on `the main FreeBSD.org
network <machines.html>`__, here are the machine resources that are
available and the sorts of work they are intended for.

You can learn more `about the FreeBSD WWW server <about.html>`__,
including the machine, the software, mirroring the FreeBSD web pages.
